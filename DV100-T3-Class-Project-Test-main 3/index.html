Indoor plants typically need watering once every 1-2 weeks. However, the watering frequency can vary based on factors like plant type, size, and environmental conditions. Always check the soil moisture level before watering to prevent overwatering.

Most outdoor plants thrive in a spot with ample sunlight, preferably receiving 6-8 hours of direct or indirect sunlight daily. However, some plants may prefer partial shade, so it's essential to consider each plant's specific sunlight requirements.

Regularly inspect your plants for signs of pests or diseases, such as wilting leaves, discolored spots, or insect activity. Keep your plants clean and dust-free, and avoid overwatering, as it can create a favorable environment for pests. Consider using natural remedies or organic pesticides to manage pest infestations while minimizing harm to beneficial insects.
